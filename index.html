Do you feel stuck, unmotivated, or overwhelmed? Is your mind constantly racing—especially at night—making it hard to rest? Do you find yourself avoiding conflict, even if it means accepting less than you deserve?

Maybe you're recovering from narcissistic abuse—whether from a partner, a family member, or in the workplace. Perhaps you're trying to find balance between work and life, or just navigating a difficult season.

Is your adolescent son or daughter in need of a mentor or healing space to talk? Are you a man seeking guidance on identity, purpose, or emotional health? Would you benefit from counseling that aligns with your Christian faith?

Whatever you're facing, you don't have to walk through it alone. Your goals matter. It's okay to ask for support — and we're here to walk alongside you on your journey.

Founder
Tanise Smith
MS, LCPC · Clinical Psychotherapist · Founder
Narcissistic Abuse RecoveryTrauma Depression & AnxietyGrief RelationshipsFaith-Based Group Therapy

Tanise is a clinically licensed psychotherapist in Kansas and Missouri and the Founder of Journey Embraced Counseling (est. 2020). With over 20 years of experience, she provides compassionate, insight-driven care for individuals navigating narcissistic abuse recovery, trauma, depression, anxiety, grief, and relationship challenges.

She was honored to be a nominated finalist for Best Therapist in the Kansas City People's Choice Awards in 2023 and 2025. She has supported hundreds of clients healing from emotionally abusive dynamics and specializes in attachment, identity development, and relational growth.

Tanise also leads specialty therapeutic groups focused on family healing, wounded identity, co-dependence recovery, and youth music-based groups. She serves as a certified clinical supervisor in Kansas and Missouri, as well as a consultant for emerging private practice clinicians.

"I look forward to sharing genuine support alongside you on your journey."

Natasha Harris-Butler
LCSW, LSCSW · Clinical Social Worker / Therapist
Anxiety & OverthinkingDepression Stress ManagementDivorce Recovery GriefCBTGroup Therapy

Natasha is a Kansas and Missouri Licensed Clinical Social Worker who has been serving individuals and families since 2006. She specializes in using Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to help clients effectively manage anxiety, overthinking, stress, and depression.

Natasha is passionate about equipping individuals with practical, evidence-based tools to challenge unhelpful thought patterns, regulate emotions, and build healthier coping strategies. She also facilitates therapeutic groups designed to address anxiety and provide structured skill-building in a supportive environment.

She is also the author of Anchored by Grace: Prayers for Keeping an Undivided Heart After Divorce, a prayer journal supporting individuals navigating the emotional and spiritual journey of divorce.

"I listen; I share empathy, understanding, and unconditional positive regard. My hope is to help clients draw from their strengths while cultivating an undivided heart."

Debra Richardson
LSCSW, LCSW · Clinical Social Worker / Psychotherapist
DepressionAnxiety Relationship ChallengesGrief & Loss CBTResilience Building

Debra is a Kansas and Missouri Licensed Clinical Social Worker and Psychotherapist with over 30 years of experience in social work and mental health. Throughout her career, she has served individuals and families in a variety of clinical settings and remains deeply committed to helping others move toward healing and wellness.

Debra views therapy as a guided conversation that creates space for growth, clarity, and meaningful change. Her approach is interactive, conversational, and grounded in Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT).

"My goal is to normalize the therapy process and provide a supportive environment where clients feel heard, empowered, and equipped to move forward with confidence."

Chris Gudjenov
LPC · Under Clinical Supervision · Adolescent Athletes & Men's Mental Health
Adolescent AthletesMen's Mental Health Anxiety & DepressionMilitary Families Substance UseMental Performance Life Transitions

Chris is a Licensed Professional Counselor (KS) practicing under clinical supervision with over 10 years of experience working with adolescents and adults. For the past seven years, he has supported U.S. Army service members and their families through resilience training, CBT-based skill development, and substance use support.

As a Certified Mental Performance Consultant, Chris specializes in working with adolescent athletes, high performers, and men navigating anxiety, depression, life transitions, and relationship challenges.

Outside of work, Chris enjoys hiking, staying active outdoors, and playing just about any sport he can get his hands on.

"Using a Cognitive-Behavioral approach, I help clients build self-awareness, manage anxiety, and develop practical tools that create lasting growth—both on and off the field."

Cynthia Stokes
LCC · Certified Life Coach / Administrative Coordinator
Adolescents & TeensWomen's Coaching Christian MentorshipLife Stress Marital ChallengesParenthood Confidence Building

Cynthia Stokes is a certified life coach with an extensive background as an instructor and mentor. She specializes in working with adolescents and teens, supporting them as they navigate emotional and mental challenges while building practical coping skills, confidence, and resilience.

She also works with women from diverse backgrounds and is especially passionate about providing Christian mentorship and coaching to women navigating life stress, marital challenges, and the journey of parenthood. As a wife of over 19 years and a mother of five, Cynthia brings both professional insight and personal experience to her work.

"I am committed to helping clients build confidence, overcome mental and emotional barriers, and move forward with greater clarity and peace."
